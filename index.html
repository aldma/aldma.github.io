About me

I am a Research Assistant at the Institute for Applied Mathematics and Scientific Computing at UniBw, under the supervision of Prof. Matthias Gerdts. Before moving to Munich, I received a B.Sc. degree in Industrial Engineering (2014) and a M.Sc. in Mechatronics Engineering (2016) from UniTn, Trento. I grew up in the Veneto countryside, Italy.

🔭 My research revolves around mathematical optimization and its applications in control systems. I am interested in computational optimization and fashinated by the interplay between numerical optimization and linear algebra. My research comprises the design, analysis, implementation, and testing of methods for continuous optimization. Currently, I am working on

↪ stabilized, primal-dual Newton-type methods for nonlinear programming;
↪ augmented Lagrangian proximal methods for constrained structured optimization.
